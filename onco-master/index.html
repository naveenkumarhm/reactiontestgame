The Technology

OncoSolutions’ patented 3D culture microtechnology presents the first 3D engineered tumor model designed and optimized as a robust, simple, and high throughput technology for pharmaceutical preclinical drug screening. It involves two aqueous polymer solutions that are immiscible at specific concentrations (similar to oil and water, but both aqueous). An immersion polyethylene glycol (PEG) polymer solution (above in purple) is dispensed into wells of a standard microplate and a denser dextran (DEX) polymer solution (above in pink) is mixed with cancer cells of interest. A liquid handling robot is used to mix the DEX phase and cancer cell solution and then dispense a submicroliter droplet of the solution into each well. This forms a 3D droplet containing cancer cells that remains intact when surrounded by the PEG phase solution.

The Team

Dr. Stephanie Ham

Co-Founder

Dr. Ham graduated with her Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering from The University of Akron in 2017 and a B.S. in Chemical Engineering from Miami University in 2012. Her dissertation work included developing and testing the 3D cancer cell model technology that became the basis for OncoSolutions. Through an extensive NSF I-Corps Teams customer discovery program, in which Dr. Ham served as the entrepeneurial lead, it became apparent that a strong unmet need exists in the cancer drug discovery market that OncoSolutions' technology could address. She has since led the formation of OncoSolutions and raised $240,000 during the company's first year. Dr. Ham has a strong background in technology development and optimization, cancer biology, and entrepreneurship.

Dr. Hossein Tavana

CTO

Dr. Tavana received his Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from University of Toronto in 2006 and served as a postdoctoral fellow at University of Michigan in 2007 developing microtechnologies that facilitate the studying of cells in biologically relevant microenvironments. Through this work, Dr. Tavana pioneered the use of novel polymeric systems for cell micropatterning applications. He joined the Biomedical Engineering department at The University of Akron in 2010 where he now serves as an associate professor and runs a Biofluid Microtechnology laboratory. He oversaw the development and optimization of OncoSolutions' technology. His innovative work has received recognition as one of the Top 13 Young Innovators in Cellular and Molecular Bioengineering and one of the Top 20 Young Investigator Frontiers in Bioengineering.

Elyse Ball, Esq.

COO

Ms. Ball completed a J.D. degree from Boston University in 2011 and now holds a position at The University of Akron Research Foundation as an assistant counsel and project manager. She is a licensed attorney in Ohio and has significant experience in technology licensing and supporting university startup companies in the polymer science and biomedical fields. She currently serves as a board member for four Ohio-based startups and leads The University of Akron's NSF I-Corps Site program. She previously worked for a business law firm and co-authored a book on patent law for scientists and engineers. Ms. Ball mentored the OncoSolutions team through the customer discovery phase and has since helped form OncoSolutions and raise funding.
